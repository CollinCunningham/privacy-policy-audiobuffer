AudioBuffer Support

How to use the app

What is AudioBuffer?

AudioBuffer is a rolling-buffer audio recorder. Rather than pressing record and hoping you caught the right moment, the app continuously holds the last N seconds of audio in memory. When something worth keeping happens, tap Save and that moment is yours.

Getting Started

Microphone Permission

On first launch, AudioBuffer will ask for access to your microphone. Tap Allow to enable recording. Without this permission the app cannot function. You can grant it later in Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone.

Starting and Stopping the Buffer

The main screen shows a slide control in the center. Drag the thumb to the right until it latches to start buffering. The control turns red and pulses while active. Drag it back to stop.

The buffer runs silently in the background. Nothing is saved to your device until you tap Save.

Saving a Clip

While the buffer is running, a Save button is visible. Tap it at any time to capture the last N seconds of audio as a new clip. A brief confirmation overlay will appear at the top of the screen.

The clip length (how many seconds are captured on each save) is adjustable in Options — see Options below.

Playback

Tap the folder icon at the bottom of the main screen to open your clips. Tap any clip to expand it and reveal the playback controls. Tap Play to listen. Selecting a different clip will automatically stop any clip currently playing.

Editing a Clip

Trimming

Open the clip list, expand a clip, and tap the pencil icon (or long-press the clip and choose Edit). The edit view shows the full waveform with two drag handles on either end:

  • Drag the left handle to set where the clip starts.
  • Drag the right handle to set where the clip ends.
  • The bright region between the handles is what will be kept.
  • Tap Play to preview your selection before committing.
  • Tap Crop to apply the trim. This overwrites the original clip.

Renaming

In the edit view, tap the clip name at the top to rename it.

Managing Clips

  • Delete a single clip: Swipe left on the clip and tap Delete, or long-press and choose Delete.
  • Share a clip: Expand the clip and tap the Share button, or long-press and choose Share. Clips are shared as standard .wav files.
  • Select multiple clips: Tap the pencil icon in the clip list to enter select mode. Tap clips to select them, then use the Share or Delete button that appears.

Options

Tap the gear icon on the main screen to open Options.

Clip Duration

Sets how many seconds of audio are captured each time you tap Save. Range: 15 – 300 seconds. Default: 30 seconds.

Clip Naming

  • Prefix: A short text label prepended to every clip name (e.g. "clip", "idea", "take"). Max 12 alphanumeric characters.
  • Timestamp naming: Names clips with the date and time they were saved — e.g. clip-2026-04-18T14-30-00.wav.
  • Numbered naming: Names clips sequentially — e.g. clip-1.wav, clip-2.wav.

Appearance

Choose between Dark, Light, or Auto (follows your device's system setting).

Background Animation

When enabled, a live waveform visualization plays behind the recording control while the buffer is active. Use the Gain slider to make the waveform larger or smaller. Turn this off if you prefer a quieter interface or want to save battery.

Tips

  • If a phone call or other interruption pauses the buffer, your previously buffered audio is still there — tap Save before navigating away to keep it.
  • Unplugging headphones or an external mic will stop the buffer. Plug back in and restart the slide control to resume.
  • Clips are stored as .wav files in the app's local storage. They are never uploaded anywhere.
